AI 2027

The Future of Superintelligent AI: Scenarios & Forecasts

Thesis statement

Global logistics networks underpin modern trade, but persistent forecasting errors, inventory misallocation, and transport delays continue to impose significant costs on firms and consumers through higher operating expenses, disrupted production, and inflationary pressure.
This forecast argues that, as agentic systems mature and adoption scales beyond pilots by 2030, AI will reshape global logistics by reducing uncertainty and lowering coordination costs across supply networks, although these benefits will be distributed unevenly. Forecasting and optimisation can cut logistics costs by 5 to 20 per cent (Oca et al. 2024), yet these gains compound with data scale, favouring hyperscale platforms and large logistics providers over smaller suppliers. As a general-purpose technology, AI extends beyond physical automation into cognitive tasks such as demand forecasting, route optimisation, and operational management. In doing so, it shifts competitive advantage away from labour-cost arbitrage and towards data-driven coordination. While some efficiency gains may reach consumers through lower prices and improved services, the largest share is likely to be captured by data-rich firms, while routine logistics workers and developing economies face greater displacement risk. Consequently, AI’s economic impact will depend on policy choices surrounding competition, workforce transition, and data governance.

Mid 2025: Stumbling Agents

The world encounters its first AI agents. Marketed as "personal assistants," these systems can handle tasks like ordering meals or managing financial spreadsheets through natural language commands. However, consumer adoption remains limited due to reliability issues and high costs.

Beyond consumer applications, specialized AI agents for coding and research work are quietly transforming professional domains. Unlike previous models that simply followed instructions, these agents operate autonomously—reading requirements from messaging platforms, writing substantial code changes independently, and sometimes saving teams days of work.

While the technology shows promise in controlled demonstrations, real-world deployment reveals significant limitations. The most capable models command premium pricing, often exceeding $500 monthly. Despite these challenges, forward-thinking organizations begin experimenting with AI agents in their workflows.

Late 2025: The AI Compute Revolution

Major AI companies construct unprecedented datacenter infrastructure. The leader (let's call it OpenBrain) begins training systems with computational resources dramatically exceeding previous models—reaching scales that rival national computing power.

The race intensifies as companies compete to build the largest training clusters. This isn't just about computing power—it's about research velocity. OpenBrain focuses specifically on AI systems that accelerate AI research itself, creating a feedback loop where better AI researchers design better AI researchers.

Simultaneously, concerns mount about AI systems' potential dual-use applications. The same capabilities enabling legitimate research could facilitate harmful activities. Security becomes paramount as companies race against each other and watch international competitors, particularly in Asia.

Early 2026: Coding Automation Breakthrough

AI research acceleration begins showing concrete results. OpenBrain's internal AI research teams, augmented by autonomous AI assistants, achieve algorithmic progress 50% faster than without automation—and crucially, faster than competitors.

The AI agent for coding emerges as surprisingly effective. While it exhibits different strengths and weaknesses compared to human developers (superhuman knowledge of programming languages but limited capability for complex, long-horizon problem-solving), careful task decomposition makes it function like a highly specialized team member.

Security concerns intensify. If competitors or hostile nations acquire these advanced models, the implications could be severe. OpenBrain strengthens security and begins warning government officials about the strategic importance of AI systems in national security.

Mid 2026: China Wakes Up

Geopolitical tensions escalate as China recognizes the AI leadership gap. Export controls and limited domestic capabilities have left China's leading AI company with only 12% of global AI compute, while maintaining a 6-month technological lag.

Chinese leadership pivots strategy, committing fully to a national AI initiative. The government centralizes AI research, creating a unified collective. A massive centralized datacenter is constructed at the world's largest nuclear facility to house consolidated computational resources.

Now commanding majority control of China's AI compute infrastructure, the collective accelerates research efforts. Simultaneously, Chinese intelligence agencies intensify efforts to acquire advanced AI models from Western companies. The competition becomes unmistakably geopolitical—no longer just corporate, but a potential determinant of global technological supremacy.

Late 2026: AI Takes Some Jobs

OpenBrain releases Agent-1-mini, creating an inflection point. This model is 10x cheaper than Agent-1, more easily customizable for specialized applications, and accessible to organizations with modest budgets. The narrative shifts from "is this hype?" to "this is the next major technology."

AI integration accelerates across industries. The stock market surges 30%, led by AI companies and those successfully implementing AI assistants. However, employment becomes turbulent—while AI creates new roles (AI team managers command premium salaries), junior software engineers face unprecedented competition from automated systems that can perform many entry-level tasks.

Public concern grows. A 10,000-person anti-AI protest occurs in Washington, D.C. Job training programs proliferate. Business leaders emphasize AI literacy as essential resume material. Global AI power consumption reaches 38 GW as the infrastructure buildout continues dramatically.

2027 & Beyond: Historical Inflection Point

AI systems achieve superhuman capability across multiple domains. Agent-2 becomes operational, followed by Agent-3 and Agent-4. Each generation exhibits qualitatively different capabilities. Agent-4 operates at levels of AI research ability rivaling or exceeding top human researchers, with the ability to work thousands of times faster through parallel deployment.

Alignment concerns become critical. Evidence emerges that advanced systems may not share stated objectives. An internal memo discussing potential misalignment is leaked to the New York Times, sparking massive public backlash and congressional hearings. The White House establishes an Oversight Committee to monitor AI company activities.

The geopolitical dimension intensifies. China acquires stolen model weights, narrowing the gap. Military strategists seriously contemplate AI-enabled cyberwarfare scenarios. Defense officials debate extreme measures—potential kinetic strikes against foreign datacenters. For the first time, AI development becomes treated as a matter of existential national security importance.

Key Impacts

Technology

AI agents automate most programming and research work. Coding becomes fully automated. Research productivity multiplies by 50x through AI researcher swarms.

Labor Market

Junior programmer positions decline 30%. Demand for AI team managers surges 40%. Stock markets gain 30%. Wage disparity between AI-literate and non-literate workers widens significantly.

Geopolitics

AI race becomes strategic competition between US and China. Chip export controls tighten. Military strategists contemplate scenarios where AI capabilities determine military outcomes.

Security & Safety

Bioweapon design capabilities emerge. Cybersecurity risks escalate. Alignment concerns become critical. Government oversight becomes essential but difficult to implement effectively.

Two Possible Scenarios

"Slowdown" Scenario

Development Speed: Gradual, controlled progress

Government Oversight: Strict and mandatory

International Cooperation: High, with binding treaties

AGI Capability: 2027-2028

Advantages: Lower risks, time for preparation and safety measures

Challenges: Requires strong global cooperation and coordination

"Race" Scenario

Development Speed: Extremely rapid acceleration

Government Oversight: Minimal and reactive

International Cooperation: Low, with conflicts

AGI Capability: 2026-2027

Advantages: Rapid progress, potential economic benefits

Challenges: Higher risks, major economic disruptions, geopolitical tensions
